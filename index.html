Personas & Storyboarding

This page is an analysis of the user experience of interacting with Brown University's mailroom kiosks. First, information about user experiences is collected through real interviews and observation then, I create storyboards about a user's experience with the kiosks.

(1) The Interface:

Hundreds of students interact with this interface every day, and there are often long lines to swipe for mail, so this interface needs to be as simple and intuitive as possible.

(2) Observations & Interviews:

I observed students using the card scanners in the Brown mailroom, and these my main takeaways.

Students Generally:

  • Swipe their card multiple times
  • Swipe their card in the wrong direction
  • Always continue into the mailroom (almost never deciding to not pickup)
  • Switch swipe-kiosks if theirs isn't working

Interview Questions & Answers

  • How do you determine which direction to swipe your card?

    • Same direction as swiping into a dorm

    • Random direction as long as the magnetic strip is inside the scanner

    • Top-to-bottom for more 'oomph'

  • How do you determine which kiosk to swipe at?

    • Whichever one is closest

    • The inside scanner, since there is less line there

  • Do you ever click the 'Cancel' button?

    • No.

  • Have you ever clicked the 'refresh' button? If so why?

    • No -- Keep swiping until it works, or move to another kiosk

    • Yes -- Card sometimes gets a partial read (only a few black dots in card textbox) -- need to clear it.

  • Does the information about the number and type of packages you have influence your decision to pickup or cancel?

    • No -- Always continue into the mailroom. I know what I ordered.

  • Why did you choose this time to pick up mail?

    • Need a package for my next class (in 10 mins) so I must go now

    • Package arrived today, picking it up as soon as possible

    • Had some free time between classes, decided to pick up mail.

(3) Personas:

Late Lucy: Lucy picks her packages up only when she needs them, often picking up books/class materials right before their associated class. She wants to get in and out of the mailroom as quickly as possible, and doesn't want to wait in line, but is rushing.

Proactive Piper: Piper is proactive and picks up her mail as soon as it arrives. She doesn't want to wait in line, but is not in a rush. She is very familiar with the mailroom systems and knows exactly what she ordered.
